Welcome to Franchise Site Intelligence
Bermuda Franchise Group · Demographic Heatmap Platform
This tool helps you identify the best locations for new club franchises using US Census demographic data. Follow the steps below to get started.
1
Get your free Census API Key REQUIRED
The app uses US Census Bureau data to power all demographic heatmaps. You need a free API key — it takes about 2 minutes to get one.
Sign up at (free, instant):
https://api.census.gov/data/key_signup.html
After signing up, check your email and click the activation link. Then come back here.
Once you have your key, scroll to the bottom of the left panel, paste it in the Census API Key field and click Save. Your key is stored in your browser and never shared.
2
Load a target market
Use the Quick Load buttons to instantly load a pre-set market, or type any city in the search box (format: City, ST — e.g. "Scottsdale, AZ"). The map will fill with color-coded census tracts showing demographic strength.
3
Read the heatmap
Dark blue = highest composite score (most ideal for a club location)
Yellow = low score
Faded/transparent = below your score threshold or filtered out

Click any colored tract to see its full demographic breakdown and score.
4
Find suggested club locations
Toggle on Suggested Club Locations at the top of the panel. Set your max locations and minimum separation, then click 🎯 Find Best Locations. The algorithm finds the optimal cluster centers — the center of gravity of wealth concentration — and packs as many viable locations into the region as possible without overlapping catchment areas.
5
Adjust the demographic filters
Use the Data Layers & Filters section to tune what makes a tract qualify:

• Household Income — slide from $100K to $200K threshold
• Median HH Income — minimum median for the tract
• Target Age Group — default 35–54, fully adjustable
• Population Density — raw population and people/sq mi filters

The Market Viability section at the bottom calculates whether a market has enough population (350K–500K+) within your radius to support 275 members.
This guide won't show again once you save your API key

This tool uses live US Census demographic data to help you identify the best locations for a new Bermuda Club. Follow the steps below to get started.

1
Get your free Census API Key
This tool pulls live data from the US Census Bureau — you need a free API key to access it. It takes about 2 minutes to get one.

→ Click here to register for your free Census API key
You'll receive an email with your key — click the activation link in that email first.
2
Paste your API Key below
Once you have your key, paste it here and click Save. It's stored in your browser and never shared.
3
Search a market
Type any US city in the search box (e.g. Scottsdale, AZ) or click a Quick Load button. The map will populate with color-coded census tracts — dark blue = highest score, yellow = lower score.

Target member profile: Age 30–54 (peak 35–49) · HHI 50K+ · Homeowner · Affluent suburban · High discretionary spending.
4
Adjust the data layers
Use the Data Layers & Filters panel to tune income thresholds, age ranges, population density, and remote worker concentration. Each layer has a weight slider. Hover the ? icons for explanations. The Remote Worker Density layer (Layer 5) identifies tracts with high work-from-home populations — your core Bermuda Club member.
5
Find the best club locations
Toggle on Suggested Club Locations and click 🎯 Find Best Locations. The algorithm identifies optimal placement centers based on wealth concentration, spacing clubs to maximize coverage without overlapping catchment areas.
6
Check Market Viability
Scroll down to Market Viability. Drop a pin and set your radius to see estimated qualified households and potential members within your catchment. Target: 275 members per location needs ~37,000 qualified HH — requires 350K–500K+ total population in a 20-mile radius.
